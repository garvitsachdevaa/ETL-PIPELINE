## Patient History

The patient is a 45-year-old male presenting with chest pain. Symptoms began three days ago and have progressively worsened. He has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes. No previous cardiac events have been recorded.

The patient is currently on metformin and lisinopril. He reports no known drug allergies. His last blood pressure reading was 148 over 92. He smokes half a pack of cigarettes per day.

## Examination Findings

Physical examination revealed mild tachycardia with a heart rate of 104 beats per minute. Blood pressure was elevated at 152 over 96. Respiratory rate was normal at 16 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation was 97 percent on room air.

Chest auscultation showed clear lung fields bilaterally. Cardiac examination revealed a regular rhythm with no murmurs. Abdominal examination was unremarkable. Lower limb examination showed no oedema.

## Diagnostic Results

ECG showed sinus tachycardia with no ST segment changes. Troponin levels were within normal limits at 0.02. Full blood count showed a mild leukocytosis of 11.4. Chest X-ray demonstrated clear lung fields with normal cardiac silhouette.

Lipid panel showed total cholesterol of 5.8 mmol per litre. LDL was elevated at 3.9 mmol per litre. HbA1c was 7.4 percent indicating suboptimal diabetic control. Renal function tests were within normal parameters.

## Treatment Plan

The patient was admitted for 24-hour cardiac monitoring and observation. Aspirin 300 mg was administered immediately on arrival. IV access was established and fluids commenced at 80 ml per hour. Serial troponin measurements were scheduled at 6-hour intervals.

Cardiology was consulted and reviewed the patient within two hours. A stress echocardiogram was arranged for the following morning. Antihypertensive medication was adjusted to include amlodipine 5 mg daily. Diabetic medication was reviewed with endocrinology input.

## Follow-up Instructions

The patient should attend the cardiac outpatient clinic within two weeks. A repeat lipid panel is required in three months. Blood pressure monitoring at home is strongly advised. Smoking cessation counselling has been arranged and referral submitted.

Dietary modifications were discussed including a low-sodium low-fat diet. The patient was advised to avoid strenuous physical activity until reviewed. A referral to the diabetic nurse specialist was made. All medications were reviewed and a updated prescription was provided on discharge.
